Personal Vehicle Projects

This page documents two hands-on automotive projects focused on engine performance and drivetrain upgrades. The first features a full twin-turbo swap on a 1996 Nissan 300ZX. The second details a rebuild of a 4.0L inline-six engine. Both involved mechanical teardown, problem solving, and real-world implementation of engineering concepts.

Project 1: 1996 Nissan 300ZX – Twin Turbo Swap

  • Original Setup: VG30DE NA, 4-speed automatic
  • New Setup: VG30DETT twin-turbo, manual transmission
  • Highlights: Full engine and transmission swap, turbo system integration, ECU adjustments

Project 2: Jeep 4.0L Inline-Six Rebuild

  • Vehicle: Jeep XJ / 4.0L inline-six
  • Work Done: Top-end rebuild, cylinder head resurfacing, valve job
  • Purpose: Restore compression and reliability, learning experience in engine internals

Conclusion

These projects highlight personal experience with real-world mechanical systems, from diagnostics and planning to precision work and final testing. Each build taught something new — from patience and precision to wiring, engine theory, and tuning fundamentals.
